ComMUSICty

Creating a community through music.

Mission

Music is a unifying factor throughout many people's lives. This underlying thread is something that can connect us all. ComMUSICty is a Girl Scout Gold Award created by Jocelyn Chin. Each week, high school volunteers work with various participants to teach them music. Currently, ComMUSICty is partnered with Lifestyles for the Disabled to teach developmentally disabled adults.

Who Are We

Jocelyn Chin

Jocelyn Chin is a Girl Scout Ambassador in Troop 5428 and is currently a high school senior. She has been a Girl Scout for over 11 years and has been serving her community through various community service projects. ComMUSICty is her Girl Scout Gold Award Project where she hopes to combine her two passions: music and helping her community.

She has been playing the flute and piano for over ten years. Along the way, many music teachers have helped to foster her growth as a musician and a person. Through this project, she hopes to impact budding musicians in the same way previous teachers have affected her.

Lifestyles for the Disabled

Lifestyles Began Operating In 1994 Through The Collaboration Of Parents, Community Organizations, Local Business People And A Group Of Developmentally Disabled Adults (Program Participants). Each Group Recognized The Need To Develop Programs That Provide Participants With Realistic Work Settings And Experiences Within The Staten Island Community And Beyond.

Having Served The Developmentally Disabled Population Of Staten Island For Over 20 Years, Lifestyles Continues To Strive To Offer More Diverse And Unique Services. Lifestyles Started As An Agency Which Served Four Adults And Families And Now Serves Over 400.
